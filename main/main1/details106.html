Monitor

LCD (Liquid Crystal Display): Common and energy-efficient. LED (Light Emitting Diode): A type of LCD that uses LED backlighting for better brightness and color. OLED (Organic Light Emitting Diode): Offers superior contrast and vibrant colors but can be more expensive. Measured diagonally in inches, common sizes range from 21 inches to 32 inches or larger. Larger screens provide a more immersive experience but require more desk space. Full HD (1920x1080): Standard for most monitors. QHD (2560x1440): Offers higher pixel density and sharper images. 4K (3840x2160): Ultra HD, excellent for gaming and content creation. 5K and above: For professional use with high detail, typically found in specialized monitors. Measured in Hertz (Hz), this indicates how many times per second the display refreshes its image. 60Hz: Minimum for general use. 120Hz-240Hz: Preferred for gaming for smoother motion. Measured in milliseconds (ms), this indicates how quickly pixels change from one color to another. Lower response times (1ms-5ms) are better for gaming to reduce motion blur. TN (Twisted Nematic): Fast response times, good for gaming, but poorer color reproduction and viewing angles. IPS (In-Plane Switching): Better color accuracy and viewing angles, ideal for graphic design and media consumption. VA (Vertical Alignment): Good contrast ratios, decent color reproduction, better for mixed usage.
