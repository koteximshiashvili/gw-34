Keyboard

Use individual mechanical switches for each key. Known for their tactile feedback and durability. Use a pressure pad system to register key presses. Generally quieter and less expensive than mechanical keyboards. Combine features of both mechanical and membrane keyboards. May use a different type of switch to provide a balance between feel and cost. Connect to devices via Bluetooth or USB receiver. Offer flexibility and reduce cable clutter. Designed to reduce strain on the wrists and hands. it comes with features like customizable RGB lighting, programmable keys, and anti-ghosting. Smaller layouts (like 60% keyboards) that omit some function keys and number pads. Ideal for portability or limited desk space.
Maintenance and Tips
Cleaning: Regularly clean your keyboard to prevent buildup of dirt and debris. Some mechanical keyboards allow for easy keycap removal. Wrist Supports: Consider using a wrist rest to improve comfort, especially during long typing sessions. Compatibility: Ensure that your keyboard is compatible with your operating system (Windows, macOS, Linux, etc.).
